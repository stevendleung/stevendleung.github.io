## Overview

Designed and built an agentic AI system that automates security detector research -- accelerating the process of investigating threat patterns and developing detection logic.

## The Challenge

Security detector development is research-intensive: it requires understanding attack patterns, exploring data, iterating on detection logic, and validating against real-world scenarios. This process is slow and requires deep domain expertise.

## Approach

- **Agentic architecture**: Built a multi-step AI agent that orchestrates research tasks -- data exploration, pattern identification, detection logic generation, and validation.
- **Context engineering**: Developed advanced context management techniques to keep the agent effective across long, complex research sessions. This included strategies for context window utilization, information prioritization, and reducing redundancy.
- **Orchestration**: Designed the system's tool-use patterns, error recovery, and task decomposition to handle the ambiguity inherent in research workflows.

## Impact

Accelerated detector research workflows and became a recognized leader in agentic AI tooling within the organization. The system demonstrated that agentic AI can meaningfully augment expert workflows -- not just automate simple tasks.

## What I Learned

The gap between "AI agent that works for me" and "AI agent that works for everyone" is enormous. As the subject matter expert, I could course-correct the system in real time. Productizing that same capability for non-experts is an order of magnitude harder -- a challenge I'm continuing to explore.
